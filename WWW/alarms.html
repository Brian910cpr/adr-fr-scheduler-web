About This County-Wide Smoke Alarm Effort

What’s happening: During Fire Prevention Week, the NC State Fire Marshal, the Columbus County Fire Marshal, local fire departments, Kidde, and the American Red Cross are distributing more than 1,000 long-life smoke alarms to 20 fire departments across Columbus County. The goal is simple: more homes protected with working alarms to help prevent future tragedies.

Read the press details

Press event: Friday, October 10, 2025 – 11:00 a.m., Klondyke-Chadbourn Fire Department, 204 North Wilson Street, Chadbourn, NC.

Speakers: Brian Taylor (NC State Fire Marshal), Shannon Blackman (Columbus County Fire Marshal), James Schott (Kidde), James O’Reilly (American Red Cross).

Focus: Use local data to locate higher-risk areas and combine education with long-life alarms. Higher-risk often includes older mobile homes, low-income residences with older adults, and neighborhoods with older housing stock.

Fire Prevention Week background

Fire Prevention Week is observed annually in October in the United States and Canada, commemorating the Great Chicago Fire. It has been officially recognized nationwide since 1925 and is supported by the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA). 2025 marks 100 years since the first Presidential proclamation of the week.
